History 17A

North America Before 1800

Colonial Encounters, Captivity, and Conflict

Course Focus: Key Themes

Expansive Colonial History

The course focuses on North America before 1800, shifting emphasis from solely American history to include the complex histories of French, British, and Spanish colonial powers and the processes of dispossession impacting Indigenous peoples.

Indigenous Captivity and Exchange

A key theme is the nature of indigenous captivity, which is characterized as non-commercial. Captives were exchanged to seal alliances, end conflicts, and serve as peace offerings, reinforcing an "economy of flesh and kinship" (Rushworth) and interdependency among nations.

The Graphic History Project (Week 10)

The Task & Goal

Working with no more than one classmate (max group size of two), you will select a particular item or source of interest from the Archives and Special Collections (in person or online databases).

Your goal is to transform your archival find into a graphic representation that serves as an engaging and accessible piece of public history.

Deliverables & Requirements

  • Graphic Representation: Create a graphic history of your choice (e.g., coloring book, placards, comic spread, T-shirts, scrapbook, original art).
  • 500-Word "Caption" Essay: Submit a museum-label-style essay explaining the project's historical significance. This must address:
    • How historians have interpreted this subject to date.
    • How your interpretation captures the significance of the event/topic, grounded in the archival evidence.
